Research Lines

1. Riemannian Isoperimetry and Optimal Inequalities in Riemannian Manifolds


Objective: In Riemannian isoperimetry, the goal is to establish results of existence, uniqueness/multiplicity, and geometric characterization for finite perimeter sets that minimize area at constant volume. The same program is intended for Hardy, Sobolev, Poincaré, Gagliardo-Nirenberg, Michael-Simon inequalities, establishing the best constants and characterizing the extremal functions. The study of the associated isoperimetric profile is also an important problem included in this research line.
Keywords: Existence of isoperimetric regions; Geometric Analysis; Best Constants; Geometric Measure Theory; Sobolev Inequalities; Nonlinear analysis; PDEs.

2. Metric Geometry


Objective: Compactness of various classes of measured metric spaces. Regularity of pointed Gromov-Hausdorff-measured limits for several classes of measured metric spaces. Application of both theories to the study of the isoperimetric profile of measured metric spaces.
Keywords: Geometric Analysis; Geometric Measure Theory; Isoperimetric profile; Regularity theory; Riemannian Geometry; Metric Geometry.

3. Nonlinear Analysis


Objective: Study of existence, uniqueness/multiplicity, and regularity of solutions to nonlinear elliptic PDEs, with emphasis on the Cahn-Hilliard equation and phase transition models.
Keywords: Calculus of Variations; Nonlinear analysis; Geometric Analysis; Infinite-dimensional Morse Theory; Nonlinear analysis; PDEs.

4. Geometric Measure Theory


Objective: To study regularity theory for currents, varifolds, and finite perimeter sets defined as solutions to geometric variational problems of interest. That is, the isoperimetric problem, Plateau’s problem, constant mean curvature hypersurfaces, and in particular, minimal surfaces.
Keywords: Calculus of Variations; Existence of isoperimetric regions; Geometric Measure Theory; Geometric Analysis; Regularity theory; Metric Geometry.

5. General Relativity


Objective: To prove, at least in particular cases using isoperimetric methods, various conjectures in general relativity, such as the Penrose inequality or the positive mass theorem in more general manifolds than those considered in the work of Schoen-Yau.
Keywords: General Relativity; Geometric Analysis; Existence of isoperimetric regions; Nonlinear analysis; PDEs.

2023 - Present Advancing New Approaches in the ACH Equation: A Research Partnership between São Paulo and UNICAM


Description: In this project, our goal is to establish the blow-up theorem for compact Riemannian manifolds with boundary. Subsequently, we will extend these results to systems with boundary and nonlocal operators in both Riemannian manifolds with and without boundary. This ambitious endeavor is expected to generate a substantial body of work, composed of at least six articles over a period of two years.
